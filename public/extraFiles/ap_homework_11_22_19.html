For each problem, please show clearly how you solve each problem.

  1. A person fires a ball directly up into the air from the ground at a speed of 20 m/s. Determine:
    1. The time it takes for the ball to rise to the top of its arc.
    2. The maximum height of the ball.
    3. The total time that the ball is in the air.
    4. The speed at which the ball strikes the ground.
  2. A person is on top of a cliff that is 30 m tall and they throw a ball 15 m/s upward. Determine:
    1. The maximum height of the ball without determining the time.
    2. The total time requires for the ball to reach the bottom of the cliff.
  3. A person is on top of a cliff that is 40 m tall and they throw a ball downward with a speed of 10 m/s. Determine:
    1. The total time required for the ball to reach the bottom of the cliff.
    2. The speed at which the ball hits the floor.
  4. A car drives horizontally off of a cliff that is 20 m tall with a speed of 40 m/s. Please solve this problem by breaking it into two different dimensions: Determine:
    1. The time that the car takes to reach the ground.
    2. The total horizontal distance the car travels before reaching the ground.
  5. A long jumper jumps horizontally off of a roof that is 10 m tall with a speed of 8 m/s. Nobody is injured. Please solve this problem by breaking it into two different dimensions: Determine:
    1. The time that the jumper takes to reach the ground.
    2. The total horizontal distance the jumper travels before reaching the ground.
